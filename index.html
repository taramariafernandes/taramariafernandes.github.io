Tara Maria Fernandes

I am a researcher, educator, and community builder whose work lives at the intersection of literary education, belonging, and the question of what books do to us — and for us — when we read them together.

I recently completed my PhD dissertation, "A Book Club in the Web of Living Literature," at Teachers College, Columbia University, where I studied how a voluntary reading community among international students at I-House becomes a space of rooting, nourishment, and shared meaning-making.

As a former teacher and researcher, I am interested in what it means to bring hope into English education, and in the radical possibilities of literary engagement outside the classroom.

Research

On literature, community, and the ethics of reading
PhD Dissertation · 2026
A Book Club in the Web of Living Literature
A post-qualitative study of the I-House Book Club at Columbia University, examining how a voluntary literary community among international graduate students creates spaces of belonging, identity, and shared literary experience. Draws on Cixous, Booth, and post-qualitative methodologies to ask what it means to animate a geography of literary hope.
Research Interests
Literary Education & Community
How do voluntary reading communities form and sustain literary life outside formal institutions? What roles do translation, migration, and multilingual identity play in how we encounter texts?

I-House Book Club

Founded October 2023 · International House, Columbia University

The I-House Book Club has been meeting since October 2023, bringing together residents and friends to read across forms, languages, and traditions. Every session ends with tea, snacks, and the question: what are you taking away from today's Book Club?
